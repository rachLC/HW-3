In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Describe in your own words how you displayed the map in different projections using QGIS

QGIS allowed me to change the coordinate reference systems of the map by clicking on the CRS button in the lower right of the QGIS platform. I could then scroll through and select new CRS settings, and thus change the projection, for the map I was working on. This enabled me to represent the same data in a variety of ways through different projections.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant- EPSG: 102016

The azimuthal projection looks similar to a conic, but is only showing a planar view of the globe. In this case the perspective is centered on the North Pole, so the projection is a top down view globe.

NSIDC EASE- Grid South- EPSG: 3974

This projection appears to be azimuthal like the previous map, but it is centered on the South Pole, rather than the North Pole. The farther away from the South Pole the greater the distortion; as evidenced by Tissot's matric circles stretching.
